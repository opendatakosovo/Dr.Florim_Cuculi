About the Dr. Florim Cuculi

Florim Cuculi is Co-Chief Physician Cardiology at the Lucerne Cantonal Hospital. Immigrated to Switzerland from Macedonia 31 years ago and worked his way up from secondary school to a doctorate.
Florim Cuculi was born in 1977 in, a small town in the mountainous west of North Macedonia. Despite poor circumstances, he had a happy, but not always easy, childhood. Because of his Albanian origins, he is seen as a "second class citizen". In addition, Florim grows up without his father - relatives have given Bajram Cuculi a job at Bau AG in Reiden. After four seasons as a guest worker, he received an annual residence permit, first brought his wife and, when the war in Yugoslavia broke out in 1991, also Florim and his brother Fatmir, who was three years his junior, to Switzerland. “I wasn't happy,” says Cuculi, “because I really wanted to study and later become a doctor. I saw this dream endangered in Switzerland. " Florim doesn't speak a word of German, can only communicate with the teachers in French on his first day of school. But his will and the fact that he has no friends give him the opportunity to invest a lot of time in learning. After just six months, he has already mastered the language, working his way from secondary school to secondary school and up to canteen. Today he is co-chief physician for cardiology at the Lucerne Cantonal Hospital. "It wasn't always easy, but it was worth it." Florim Cuculi presses the button with 14, the elevator door closes. The ward of the privately insured is due to visit an elderly patient who was in intensive care yesterday. "He had water on his lungs, it came from a leaky heart valve," says Cuculi, before knocking on the door and entering the room. «There are many roads leading to Rome», he explains the possibilities to the patient and their relatives before proposing the best variant from his point of view. The cell phone rings - once more - Florim Cuculi is a sought-after man. "All right, prepare everything," he speaks into the phone and walks out the door into the corridor. "An emergency, suspected heart attack." On the way to the interns' office, Cuculi meets a patient whose heart he drew half a liter of water a week ago. The two clap, a kiss on the left, a kiss on the right, exchange a few words in Albanian. Then it goes on, back to the elevator, 2nd floor. Florim Cuculi has traveled a lot, studied in Basel and Oxford, and did internships overseas. He speaks fluent German, English and Albanian, with broken French, Serbo-Croatian, Macedonian. He has a wife and two sons, a house in Geuensee. "Homeland?" he repeats the question and ponders for a long time, “Home is a difficult word. The older I get, the less I know where I really am at home. " In the past he really wanted to be “more Swiss than the Swiss, to belong. I stopped doing that. Today I just want to be Florim. " In certain situations he is Swiss, in others Albanian. "The greeting to the patient with a handshake," he says of the encounter in the corridor, "was very personal and warm, typically Albanian." On the other hand, Cuculi describes the conversation with the elderly man and his relatives as Swiss. Pros and cons weighed, dry, structured, sober. «I often combine in everyday life, am neither completely one nor the other, that is part of my success as a doctor: the combination of empathy and precision. " What also contributes to his success is perseverance. His weekly workload is 70 hours, often even 80. He recently opened a cardiology practice in the city in collaboration with the Lucerne Cantonal Hospital. There, in addition to the interventions in the hospital, he carries out cardiac examinations, meetings, EKGs and follow-up examinations.
